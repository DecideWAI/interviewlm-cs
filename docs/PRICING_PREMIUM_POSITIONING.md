# InterviewLM: Premium Positioning Pricing Model ($15-20/Assessment)

## Executive Summary

**Strategic Shift**: Moving from $10 to $15-20 base pricing with heavy COGS analysis ($2-4/assessment) to achieve premium market positioning while maintaining 75-85% gross margins.

**Why Premium Positioning?**
- Current COGS of $2-4 provides significant margin cushion
- Premium positioning attracts better customer segments
- Enterprise deals require premium pricing for credibility
- Competitive with premium players (Codility $10-20, HackerRank $16-24)
- Enables profit-driven growth instead of volume-driven growth

**Bottom Line**: This strategy transforms InterviewLM from a cost-leader to a premium solution, increasing profitability by 3-5x while actually improving customer quality and NRR.

### Pricing Logic by Tier

#### Pay-as-You-Go: $20/assessment
- **Target**: Trial users, initial explorers (0-5 assessments/month)
- **Margin**: 85% ($17 profit/$20 revenue when COGS=$3)
- **Psychology**: Anchor price - premium positioning from start
- **Conversion path**: → Small Pack within 1-2 months
- **Value prop**: "Try before committing, premium quality"

#### Small Pack: 10 credits @ $180 ($18/credit) - 10% Discount
- **Target**: Small teams, single hiring manager (5-10 assessments/month)
- **Monthly equivalent**: ~$180 spend (11% cheaper than PAYG)
- **Margin**: 83% ($150 profit)
- **Commitment signal**: First purchase lock-in at $180
- **Use case**: Initial screening pipeline, 1-2 open positions
- **Psychology**: Entry-level volume purchase with perceived savings

#### Medium Pack: 50 credits @ $750 ($15/credit) - 25% Discount
- **Target**: Growing teams, active hiring (40-60 assessments/month)
- **Monthly equivalent**: ~$750 spend
- **Margin**: 80% ($600 profit)
- **Sweet spot**: Maximum customer value without large commitment
- **Use case**: Sustained hiring pipeline, multiple positions
- **Psychology**: 25% savings ($250/month vs PAYG) strongly motivates upgrade
- **Strategic value**: Captures 70% of SMB market

#### Large Pack: 200 credits @ $2,400 ($12/credit) - 40% Discount
- **Target**: Mid-market, scale-up hiring (150-250 assessments/month)
- **Monthly equivalent**: ~$2,400 spend
- **Margin**: 75% ($1,800 profit)
- **Large commitment**: $2,400 one-time investment signals serious intent
- **Switching cost**: Credits must be used, increases loyalty
- **Use case**: High-volume recruiting, multiple departments
- **Psychology**: 40% savings ($800/month vs PAYG) justifies large upfront spend

#### Enterprise Pack: 500+ credits @ $5,000+ ($10/credit) - 50% Discount
- **Target**: Large enterprises, ongoing operations (500+ assessments/month)
- **Monthly equivalent**: $5,000+ baseline
- **Margin**: 70% ($3,500+ profit)
- **Custom pricing**: Volume negotiation above 50% discount only in special cases
- **Strategic advantages**:
  - Dedicated support tier
  - Custom integrations
  - SLA guarantees
  - API access
  - Volume rebates (5% additional discount for annual prepay)
- **Use case**: Continuous hiring, multiple business units

## PART 5: PSYCHOLOGICAL PRICING STRATEGIES

### 1. Anchor Pricing (Starting at $20)

**Strategy**: Lead with $20/assessment as the anchor price
- Shows premium positioning from first glance
- Makes discounted tiers look like exceptional value
- $18 feels like a deal compared to $20
- $15 feels like major savings

**Implementation**:
- Homepage: "Starting at $20 per assessment"
- Pricing table: Show PAYG at $20 first
- CTA buttons: "$20 assessment" for PAYG

### 2. Decoy Pricing (Make Medium Pack Look Attractive)

**Problem**: Without decoys, customers might pick smallest or largest pack (missing sweet spot)

**Solution**: Use strategic decoys

```
SHOWN ON PRICING PAGE:

Small Pack
10 credits @ $180/mo
Most popular for: Startups

MEDIUM PACK ← DECOY MAKES THIS STAND OUT
50 credits @ $750/mo
Most popular for: SMBs
✓ Save $250/month vs Small
✓ 3.5x more credits for 4x price

Large Pack
200 credits @ $2,400/mo
Most popular for: Scale-ups
```

**Psychology**:
- Large Pack looks expensive at $2,400
- Small Pack looks limiting at 10 credits
- Medium Pack is "the sweet spot" - not cheapest, not most expensive
- Visual hierarchy: Medium highlighted in color

### 3. Price Framing (.00 vs .99 vs .95)

**Best Practice for Premium SaaS**: Use .00 (not .99)
- $20.00 sounds premium
- $19.99 sounds discounted/cheap
- Use whole dollars to signal confidence

**Discounts framed as percentages, not amounts**:
- "25% off" (better than "save $5")
- "40% savings" (better than "pay $8 instead of $12")
- Percentage signals bigger savings than absolute value

### 4. Anchoring Alternatives (Show What You Save)

**On pricing page, show comparison table:**

```
Running 100 Assessments/Month:

Traditional Recruiting (per assessment):
Manual screening: $50-100/assessment
Time lost: $200-300/assessment
TOTAL: $250-400 per assessment

InterviewLM:

- Reduces time by 80%
- Improves hire quality by 40%

YOUR SAVINGS: $235-385 per assessment
ANNUAL SAVINGS: $282,000 - $462,000
```

This anchors the assessment price to the problem it solves, not just cost.

### 5. Social Proof Pricing

**On pricing page:**

```
Small Pack: 10 credits @ $180/mo
↑ Popular with: Single hiring managers, small recruiters

MEDIUM PACK: 50 credits @ $750/mo
↑ MOST POPULAR - 65% of customers choose this
Most popular with: Growth teams, startups
```

## PART 9: RISK MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Customer Churn from Price Increase

**Probability**: Medium-High
**Impact**: High (revenue loss)

**Mitigation Strategies**:
1. **Grandfathering**: Existing customers locked at old price for 12 months
2. **Value Communication**: Emphasize improved AI, faster results, better accuracy
3. **Flexible Migration**: Offer middle-ground pricing ($12.50-15) for sensitive customers
4. **Retention Offers**: Free month, bonus credits for long-term contracts

**Acceptable Churn**: Up to 15% from pricing alone is acceptable given 100% revenue increase

### Risk 2: Competitive Price Response

**Risk**: Competitors match or undercut pricing

**Response Playbook**:
1. **Don't race to bottom** - Quality/differentiation focus
2. **Add value**: Premium features, better support
3. **Data-driven pricing**: Use analytics to justify higher price
4. **Segment pricing**: Different prices for different customer types

### Risk 3: Enterprise Deals Stall

**Risk**: Large customers push back on 50% discount reduction

**Mitigation**:
1. Show ROI: "This saves hiring costs by $X"
2. Offer volume rebates: 5% additional discount for $100K+ annual
3. Flexible terms: Quarterly or extended payment plans
4. Custom packages: White-label, SSO, integrations at premium

### Risk 4: Margin Squeeze if COGS Rises Above $4

**Risk**: COGS increases to $5+, margins collapse

**Mitigation**:
1. **Infrastructure optimization**: Batch assessments, cache results
2. **Model efficiency**: Fine-tune Claude prompts to reduce tokens
3. **Tiered models**: Use cheaper models for screening, premium for deep analysis
4. **Volume negotiations**: Lock in AI/compute costs at scale
