## SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING PLAYBOOK

### For Sales Team

#### Objection 1: "This is a 100% price increase. That's too much."

**Root Cause**: Customer feels blindsided by magnitude

**Response Framework**:
1. **Acknowledge**: "I understand price increases feel big"
2. **Context**: "But let's compare to what you'd pay competitors"
3. **Show Value**: Create quick ROI calculation
4. **Offer Flexibility**: Suggest tiering down or custom pricing

**Sample Script**:
```
"Great question. Here's how we think about it:

HackerRank charges $15-25 per assessment.
Codility charges $12-20 per assessment.
We're charging $12-20, so we're actually matching premium players.

The reason: We've significantly improved our AI evaluation engine,
reduced setup time to 5 minutes, and added advanced features.

For your use case running 50 assessments/month:
- HackerRank: $750-1,250/month = $9,000-15,000/year
- Codility: $600-1,000/month = $7,200-12,000/year
- InterviewLM Medium Pack: $750/month = $9,000/year

We're actually competitive or cheaper while being faster to setup.

What matters most to you: price, speed, or evaluation quality?"
```

#### Objection 2: "My budget doesn't support this. I'll go with competitor X."

**Root Cause**: Customer sees this as pure cost vs alternative

**Response Framework**:
1. **Diagnose**: Which competitor? Why are they cheaper?
2. **Reframe**: Move from cost to value/ROI
3. **Quantify**: Show ROI on hiring quality
4. **Offer Path**: Suggest lower tier or payment plan

**Sample Script**:
```
"Understood. Before you go, let me show you something.

If you're comparing to TestDome at $5/assessment:
- They offer basic screening
- Setup takes 2-3 days
- No soft skills evaluation

Our Medium Pack at $15/assessment:
- Includes AI-powered evaluation
- Setup in 5 minutes
- Evaluates technical skills + AI/tool proficiency + soft skills

ROI Calculation:
One bad engineer hire costs $50K-200K in productivity loss.
One-day faster hiring = $2K-5K value per hire.

For your 50 hires/year:
- Extra bad hires prevented: 2-3 per year = $100K-600K savings
- Faster hiring: 50 hires × $5K = $250K value
- Total value: $350K-850K

Your cost: $9,000/year on Medium Pack
Your savings: $350K+
ROI: 38x-94x

Can we talk about this in terms of hiring outcomes vs cost?"
```

#### Objection 3: "We've been on $10 pricing. Why do you get to increase without giving us something?"

**Root Cause**: Existing customer feels left behind/penalized

**Response Framework**:
1. **Validate**: "You're right, you've been a loyal customer"
2. **Explain**: "Here's what you're getting for new price"
3. **Sweeten**: "Let me offer you..."
4. **Transition**: Clear path to upgrade

**Sample Script**:
```
"You're absolutely right, and I appreciate your loyalty.

Over the past [X months], we've significantly improved the platform:
- Claude 4.5 engine is 30% more accurate
- Setup went from 30 min to 5 minutes
- New soft skills evaluation feature
- Advanced reporting for Enterprise customers

These improvements cost us to build, hence the pricing adjustment.

To show our appreciation for your loyalty, here's what I'm offering:

Option 1: Stay on current pricing through [DATE], then transition to new pricing
Option 2: Upgrade early, and get 1 month free + bonus $200 in credits
Option 3: Custom pricing - let's discuss what works for your budget

Which feels fairest to you?"
```

#### Objection 4: "Can you give us a discount to stay?"

**Root Cause**: Customer is price-sensitive and wants deal

**Response Framework**:
1. **Avoid**: Racing to bottom on price (kills profitability)
2. **Redirect**: To value or alternative tiers
3. **Empower**: Show pricing is structured to avoid discounting
4. **Offer**: Alternative value (support, features, term discount)

**Sample Script**:
```
"I appreciate you asking. Here's my position:

Our pricing is structured to be sustainable long-term.
At every tier, we maintain margins to invest in product.

HackerRank, Codility, and LeetCode don't discount their base pricing either.
They compete on feature sets, not price.

That said, here's how I can help:

If budget is the issue:
- Start with Small Pack ($180/month) and upgrade as you grow
- Switch to monthly subscription instead of prepaid (more flexible)
- Commit to annual and get 20% off

If it's about value:
- Let me show you ROI calculations specific to your hiring
- Introduce you to customer success team for premium support
- Discuss custom feature development

What sounds most helpful?"
```

#### Objection 5: "You're losing sight of SMBs with this pricing."

**Root Cause**: Customer thinks premium positioning excludes small businesses

**Response Framework**:
1. **Validate**: "We care about SMBs"
2. **Show**: Small Pack tier specifically designed for them

## SECTION 6: RISK MITIGATION PLAYBOOK

### Risk 1: Excessive Customer Churn

**Warning Signs**:
- >10% monthly churn rate
- Churn concentrated in Medium/Large packs
- Explicit pricing complaints in churn reasons

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. **Pause new pricing for 2 weeks**: Assess situation
2. **Offer bridge pricing**: 85% of new price for 3-6 months
3. **Accelerate feature rollout**: Show value justifying premium price
4. **Segment marketing**: Focus on quality-conscious buyers, not price-sensitive

**Decision Point**: If churn >15% in Month 2, revise pricing downward to $12-15 range

### Risk 2: Enterprise Deals Fall Through

**Warning Signs**:
- >2 Enterprise RFPs lose to pricing
- Multiple sales team complaints about price objections
- Lost deals cite "too expensive vs Codility"

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. **Negotiate custom pricing**: Offer 20-30% discount for annual contracts
2. **Bundle services**: Add dedicated support, custom integrations at premium
3. **Volume rebates**: Show path to 10% additional discount at 2000+ assessments/year
4. **Multi-year discounts**: Offer better pricing for 2-3 year commitments

**Decision Point**: If 20%+ of qualified opportunities are lost to price, implement strategic discounting

### Risk 3: Competitive Price Response

**Warning Signs**:
- HackerRank, Codility announce price cuts
- Customer feedback mentions competitor offers
- Sales lose 3+ deals specifically to competitor undercutting

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. **Don't match price cuts**: Emphasize quality/speed differentiation
2. **Add premium features**: Justify pricing with enhanced evaluation engine
3. **Segment strategy**: Premium tier keeps high price, create "standard" tier at $12
4. **Customer success**: Invest in NRR to retain existing customers

**Decision Point**: If 20%+ of opportunities cite competitor pricing, launch "standard tier" at $12-15

### Risk 4: Billing/Technical Issues

**Warning Signs**:
- >2% payment failures on prepaid packs
- Billing disputes from customers
- Support team overwhelmed with billing questions

**Mitigation Actions**:
1. **Test billing thoroughly**: Pre-launch comprehensive testing
2. **Dedicated support person**: Have one person focused on billing issues
3. **FAQ ready**: Most common billing questions answered in support portal
4. **Escalation path**: Clear process for complex billing issues

**Decision Point**: If >3% payment failure rate, pause premium pricing launch until fixed
